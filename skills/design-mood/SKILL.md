# Design: Mood

Brief: **$ARGUMENTS** — first word(s) are the target mood; the rest is what to design. If no mood was given, ask: *"When someone lands on this, what should they feel in the first three seconds?"* — then map their answer to the nearest mood below.

Foundation rules from `design-dna` apply. This skill converts an **emotion** into dial positions: every mood is a specific recipe of hue temperature, contrast, type voice, shape, pace, and texture.

## How emotion maps to design (the mechanics)

| Lever | What it controls |
|---|---|
| Hue & temperature | Warm = approachable, alive · Cool = competent, calm · Desaturated = serious, past · Saturated = energy, present |
| Contrast | High = drama, urgency, confidence · Low = softness, rest, intimacy |
| Shape | Round = safe, friendly · Sharp = precise, serious · Organic/irregular = natural, human |
| Type voice | Serif = heritage, thought · Geometric sans = modern, exact · Rounded = warm · Mono = technical, honest |
| Pace (motion + density) | Fast/snappy = energy · Slow/sparse = luxury, calm · Static = gravity |
| Texture | Grain/paper = human, past · Flat = digital, now · Glow = future |

A mood fails when ONE lever contradicts the others (springy bounce in a "calm" design; pure sharp gray in a "cozy" one). Consistency across all six levers is what makes the feeling land.

## Procedure

1. Read the target mood's full recipe in [moods.md](moods.md) — palette, type, shape, motion, texture, copy voice, and its "never" list.
2. State the recipe back as the design contract before building.
3. Build with the recipe applied to ALL six levers, not just color.
4. Self-check with the mood's litmus question (each entry has one), plus the `design-dna` swap test.

## Available moods

**calm** · **energetic** · **trustworthy** · **nostalgic** · **futuristic** · **organic** · **somber** · **urgent** · **dreamy** · **cozy** — full recipes in [moods.md](moods.md).

If the requested emotion isn't listed (e.g. "rebellious", "mysterious"), derive a recipe yourself using the six-lever table, write it out as a contract in the same format, and proceed — the listed moods show the expected depth.

Blends are allowed but ordered: pick a dominant mood (the structure) and a secondary (an accent flavor), e.g. *trustworthy with a warm/cozy accent* for a family-finance app. Never blend opposites equally — calm-urgent is no mood at all.
