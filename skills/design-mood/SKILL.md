# Design: Mood

Brief: **$ARGUMENTS** - first word(s) are the target mood; the rest is what to design.
No mood given? Ask: "When someone lands on this, what should they feel in the first three seconds?" and map the answer to the nearest mood.

Foundation rules from `design-dna` apply.
A mood is a recipe across six levers - hue/temperature, contrast, shape, type voice, pace, texture - and it fails when one lever contradicts the others (springy bounce in a "calm" design); consistency across all six is what makes the feeling land.

## Procedure

1. Read the target mood's full recipe in [moods.md](moods.md): palette, type, shape, motion, texture, copy voice, never list.
2. State the recipe back as the design contract before building.
3. Build with the recipe applied to ALL six levers, not just color.
4. Self-check with the mood's litmus question, plus the `design-dna` swap test.

Moods: **calm · energetic · trustworthy · nostalgic · futuristic · organic · somber · urgent · dreamy · cozy**.
An unlisted emotion ("rebellious") gets derived from the six-lever table at the top of [moods.md](moods.md), in the same contract format.
A concrete scene or occasion (sunrise, rainy, wedding) belongs to `design-scene` instead.
Blends are ordered: a dominant mood for structure, a secondary as accent; never blend opposites equally.
