# Design: Scene

Brief: **$ARGUMENTS** — first word(s) are the scene; the rest is what to design. If no scene was given, ask: *"If this product were a moment — a time, a weather, an occasion — which one?"* — then map their answer to the nearest scene below.

Foundation rules from `design-dna` apply. Where `design-mood` starts from an **emotion**, this skill starts from a **moment everyone has lived** — which is why it works: nobody has to be told what sunrise light looks like or how quiet snowfall is. The scene is a memory the user already has; the design's job is to trigger it.

## How a scene becomes a design (the mechanics)

A scene is remembered through four senses. Interrogate each one and it sets the dials:

| Sense | Ask of the scene | What it sets |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Where does it come from? How warm? How hard? | Palette, gradient direction, shadow style, dark/light base |
| **Air** | Clear, misty, rainy, snowy, smoky? | Texture, grain, blur, transparency |
| **Soundtrack** | What's playing, and how fast? | Motion speed, easing, scroll rhythm |
| **Stakes** | Who's watching? Ceremony, play, or evaluation? | Type voice, formality, copy register, density |

**Light is the master lever.** It alone separates the time-of-day scenes: sunrise = warmth rising from the bottom edge into cool sky; sunset = warmth sinking from the top into dusk; noon = flat light, hard short shadows, no gradient at all; midnight = darkness with pinpoint lights. Get the light wrong and no amount of styling saves the scene.

A scene fails the same way a mood does: one contradicting lever (hard noon shadows in a rainy design; bouncy springs in a winter one) breaks the memory. Consistency across all four senses is what makes someone *recognize* the moment instead of reading about it.

## Procedure

1. Read the target scene's full recipe in [scenes.md](scenes.md) — color, type, shape, space/pace, motion, texture, copy voice, and its "never" list.
2. State the recipe back as the design contract before building.
3. Build with the recipe applied to ALL levers, not just the palette.
4. Self-check with the scene's litmus question (each entry has one), plus the `design-dna` swap test.

The listed scenes show the expected depth.

## Routing & blends

- Brief names an **emotion** (calm, urgent, cozy…) → `design-mood`. Names a **style** (minimal, brutalist, luxe…) → that style skill. Names a **moment** → you're in the right place.
- Scenes take a mood accent the same way moods blend: pick the scene as structure and the mood as flavor — *rainy with a trustworthy accent* for a focused-work app that handles money. Never blend opposing light: sunrise-midnight is no scene at all.
