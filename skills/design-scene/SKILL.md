# Design: Scene

Brief: **$ARGUMENTS** - first word(s) are the scene; the rest is what to design.
No scene given? Ask: "If this product were a moment - a time, a weather, an occasion - which one?" and map the answer to the nearest scene.

Foundation rules from `design-dna` apply.
The scene is a memory the user already has; the design's job is to trigger it through four senses - light, air, soundtrack, stakes.
**Light is the master lever**: get it wrong and no styling saves the scene.
One contradicting lever (bouncy springs in a winter design) breaks the memory.

## Procedure

1. Read the target scene's full recipe in [scenes.md](scenes.md): color, type, shape, space/pace, motion, texture, copy voice, never list.
2. State the recipe back as the design contract before building.
3. Build with the recipe applied to ALL levers, not just the palette.
4. Self-check with the scene's litmus question, plus the `design-dna` swap test.

Scenes: **sunrise · sunset · sunny · rainy · winter · midnight · festival · wedding · interview · thesis**.
An unlisted scene (monsoon, campfire, graduation) gets derived from the four-senses table at the top of [scenes.md](scenes.md), in the same format with a never list and litmus question.
Scenes take a mood accent (dominant scene, mood flavor); never blend opposing light - sunrise-midnight is no scene at all.
