# Scene recipes for /design-scene

Each recipe opens with the scene itself (the sensory anchor), then sets all six levers: color, type, shape, space/pace, motion, texture - plus copy voice, kin (nearest moods/styles), and a litmus question. Hex values are starting points; tune to the product, keep the relationships.

## Deriving an unlisted scene: the four senses

| Sense | Ask of the scene | What it sets |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Where does it come from? How warm? How hard? | Palette, gradient direction, shadow style, dark/light base |
| **Air** | Clear, misty, rainy, snowy, smoky? | Texture, grain, blur, transparency |
| **Soundtrack** | What's playing, and how fast? | Motion speed, easing, scroll rhythm |
| **Stakes** | Who's watching - ceremony, play, or evaluation? | Type voice, formality, copy register, density |

Light examples: sunrise = warmth rising from the bottom into cool sky; sunset = warmth sinking from the top into dusk; noon = flat light, hard short shadows; midnight = darkness with pinpoint lights.
Write the derived recipe in the same format as the entries below, including the never list and a litmus question.

---

## sunrise
*For: onboarding, morning/habit apps, day-one launches, fresh-start campaigns, January products, health resets.*
- **Scene:** 6:10am. Cool blue air, first warmth at the horizon, the day still unwritten.
- **Color:** light rises from below - apricot `#FFC9A0` and pale gold `#FFE9C4` at the bottom edge dissolving up into morning blue `#D8E6F2`; sky-white `#FBFDFF` base; ink dawn-navy `#22304A`; one sun accent `#F2A35C` for the primary action.
- **Type:** light, open, optimistic - humanist sans (**Schibsted Grotesk**, **Albert Sans**) at weight 300–400 for display, regular for body; generous air around everything.
- **Shape:** the horizon arc - wide shallow SVG curves as section dividers, a sun-disc circle as the only decorative shape, 12–20px radii.
- **Space/pace:** open sky - huge top padding in the hero, sections gently gaining density down the page (the day filling in).
- **Motion:** everything rises - reveals translate up 16–24px over 500ms ease-out; the hero gradient warms perceptibly once on load (cool → warm over ~1.2s). Nothing descends from above.
- **Texture:** fine grain inside gradients; thin white mist bands at 3–5% near the horizon line.
- **Copy:** future tense, quiet optimism, second person. "Day one starts quiet." "Begin before the noise."
- **Kin:** `calm` × `dreamy` with a hopeful pulse.
- **Never:** midnight palettes, urgency reds, dense first screens, drop-down entrances, neon.
- **Litmus:** does the first screen feel like 6am - cool, quiet, and about to be warm?

## sunset
*For: year-in-review, evening/wind-down modes, journals, farewells and sign-offs, portfolio outros, retrospectives.*
- **Scene:** 6:47pm. Golden hour. Long shadows, warm light sinking into dusk.
- **Color:** sunrise inverted - ember band on top (burnt coral `#E96E4C`, amber `#F2A65A`, rose-dust `#D98E8E`) dissolving down into dusk violet-charcoal `#2E2438`; the page darkens as you scroll. Shadows long, soft, warm-tinted, offset down-right.
- **Type:** a warm serif with some age - **Fraunces** (soft axis, high optical size), **Source Serif 4**; italics for remembered lines.
- **Shape:** low horizons - wide full-bleed bands, a half-set sun-disc peeking from behind section edges as the motif.
- **Space/pace:** a slow descent - long sections, each screen calmer than the last; the final screen nearly empty (the afterglow).
- **Motion:** settle and fade - elements ease down 8px into rest (never up) over 500–700ms; crossfades between sections; optionally a gradient that drifts on a 60s loop.
- **Texture:** golden grain heavier than sunrise (6–8% in gradient bands), warm vignette on imagery.
- **Copy:** reflective past tense, gratitude without saccharine. "Here's what the year held." "That was the good part."
- **Kin:** `nostalgic` × `calm` - the closing-credits register.
- **Never:** bright white backgrounds, "get started" CTAs, count-up dashboards, cold blues.
- **Litmus:** does every element look like it was photographed at golden hour?

## sunny
*For: travel, summer campaigns, outdoor and kids' products, sports days, markets, ice cream.*
- **Scene:** 12 noon, zero clouds. Hard short shadows, full color, sprinklers somewhere.
- **Color:** few but at full saturation - sky cobalt `#3D9BE9`, sun yellow `#FFD23F`, grass green `#62B65F` on pure white; that's the whole palette. Shadows are the tell: solid, short, hard-edged (4–6px offset, no blur, ink at 15%).
- **Type:** rounded and confident - **Outfit** or **Baloo 2** for display at bold weights and big sizes; sturdy sans body.
- **Shape:** crisp rounded (10–14px), sticker-adjacent; sun rays and scalloped circles as motifs.
- **Space/pace:** open-air gaps, bright and orderly; rhythm bouncy but never cluttered.
- **Motion:** quick and clean - 200ms pop-ins with a tiny scale; nothing slow, hazy, or drifting (there is no fog at noon).
- **Texture:** none. Clear air, flat color - the defining absence.
- **Copy:** open invitations, first person plural. "Come outside." "We're open all summer."
- **Kin:** `energetic` × `playful`, with cleaner air than either.
- **Never:** gradients (noon light is flat), grain or mist, dark mode, corporate navy, tiny gray text.
- **Litmus:** does it feel like squinting - in a good way?

## rainy
*For: reading and writing tools, focus and lo-fi apps, tea/coffee brands, libraries, blogs, slow commerce.*
- **Scene:** steady rain on the window. Grey light outside, lamp on inside. You're on the good side of the glass.
- **Color:** overcast greys with blue in them - window grey `#AEBCC4`, cloud `#DFE5E8`, wet slate `#5C6B74`; ink blue-grey `#2B3640`; ONE lamp accent, amber `#D9A45B`, reserved for links and the primary action - the lit window in the rain.
- **Type:** bookish - **Literata** or **Lora** for text at 17–18px with 1.7 leading (reading weather); a quiet sans for UI.
- **Shape:** softened rectangles (8–12px); thin internal dividers on grids like window mullions.
- **Space/pace:** warm interior, grey exterior - content in a snug column (~680–720px), backgrounds wide and empty like the view outside.
- **Motion:** drip-slow - 400–600ms fades, occasional slow vertical drift; never literal animated rain (at most one decorative pane with 2% diagonal hatching).
- **Texture:** wet glass - frosted backdrop blur on overlays, faint condensation gradients at the edges; paper warmth inside cards against the cool outside.
- **Copy:** interior voice, present tense, unhurried. "Stay in. Read something."
- **Kin:** `cozy` × `calm` with a drop of `somber` - the introvert register.
- **Never:** hard shadows (there is no sun), saturated color beyond the one lamp, urgency mechanics, springy motion.
- **Litmus:** does the page feel like being on the warm side of the window?

## winter
*For: seasonal/holiday pages, premium minimal portfolios, archives, outdoor-gear and watch brands, quiet luxury.*
- **Scene:** fresh snowfall. Sound absorbed, air crisp, one cardinal on a bare branch.
- **Color:** snow field - `#F7FAFC`, ice `#DCE9F2`, pale shadow-blue `#B8CCDC`; bare-branch charcoal `#2A2E33` for ink; ONE berry accent `#B33A3A` spent exactly once per page (the cardinal in the snow).
- **Type:** crisp and thin like cold air - **Cormorant Garamond** light for an elegant register or **Space Grotesk** light for a modern one; slightly wide tracking; high contrast between display and body.
- **Shape:** hairline precision - 1px frost-blue borders, 0–6px radius, crystalline geometry (a subtle diamond or six-fold motif at most).
- **Space/pace:** the most whitespace of any scene - snow *is* whitespace; one element per region; 140–180px of silence between sections.
- **Motion:** near-stillness - reveals as slow 600ms fades; ONE drifting element allowed (8–12s, a single snowflake, not a flurry). Cold things don't bounce.
- **Texture:** frost grain at 2%, a breath-fog white radial softening the edges.
- **Copy:** spare, hushed, declarative. "Quiet season. New work in January."
- **Kin:** `calm` × `somber`'s restraint × `luxe`'s withholding, colder than all three.
- **Never:** warm beige (that's autumn), a second red, dense grids, jolly clutter - winter is not a Christmas sweater.
- **Litmus:** does the page absorb sound the way fresh snow does?

## midnight
*For: dev tools, late-night writing and music production, astronomy, focus modes, night-shift dashboards.*
- **Scene:** 2am. The world asleep, you awake. Desk lamp, dark room, total focus.
- **Color:** true night - blue-black `#0B0E17` (never grey-black), moonlit text `#D9E0EA` at ~90% opacity; city-light accents as pinpoints only (window-amber `#E8B45A`, teal `#4FD8C4` on dots, ticks, carets - never floods). Pure `#FFFFFF` is banned: it wakes the room.
- **Type:** **JetBrains Mono** for data, a quiet sans (**Geist**, **IBM Plex Sans**) for prose; modest sizes - nothing shouts at night.
- **Shape:** low relief - borders at 8–12% white, 6–10px radius, elevation expressed as *slightly less black*, not shadows.
- **Space/pace:** pools of light - content islands surrounded by generous darkness; bright only where the work is (the desk-lamp pattern).
- **Motion:** dim and smooth - 150–250ms opacity/transform; a blinking caret or one slow-pulsing status dot as the only signs of life.
- **Texture:** starfield restraint - a few 1px light dots at 4% in empty regions, faint vignette toward the edges.
- **Copy:** lowercase, terse, companionable. "still here. autosaved 2 min ago."
- **Kin:** `futuristic`'s gear at `calm`'s pulse.
- **Never:** white-flash modals, embedded light-mode screenshots, full-saturation alarm reds, busy gradients.
- **Litmus:** could you use it at 2am without waking anyone?

## festival
*For: launches, event and concert pages, sale seasons, Diwali/Holi/New Year campaigns, community celebrations, birthdays.*
- **Scene:** lights strung against the night sky, music from somewhere, color everywhere on purpose.
- **Color:** the one scene where many colors is correct - marigold `#FFB300`, fuchsia `#E84393`, electric violet `#7C4DFF`, flame `#FF6B35` popping off deep night plum `#1A1026`; the ground stays dark so every light reads as a light. (Daytime variant: the same brights on warm cream - Holi at noon.)
- **Type:** exuberant display with real character - **Clash Display** or **Cabinet Grotesk** at heavy weights and huge sizes; ±2–3° rotations for energy.
- **Shape:** bunting and garlands - triangle or scallop trims on section edges, pill badges, geometric confetti (circles, squares, zigzags) placed deliberately, never scattered at random.
- **Space/pace:** dense and rhythmic - loud sections alternating with quiet breaths; the breaths matter (every festival has lulls between acts).
- **Motion:** the celebratory lever - staggered pop-ins with overshoot (300–400ms spring), a garland sway on a 3–4s loop, count-up numbers, and at most ONE confetti burst on the key success moment. Ambient confetti is noise, not joy.
- **Texture:** paper-cut layering (hard offset shadows behind shapes), lantern glow (radial gradients at ~20% behind the lights).
- **Copy:** exclamation marks are *earned* here - warm, plural, inviting. "You're invited." "Tonight, we celebrate."
- **Kin:** `energetic` × `playful`, after dark; add `nostalgic` for heritage festivals.
- **Never:** beige restraint, single-accent minimalism, grey anything, fake countdown urgency dressed up as fun.
- **Litmus:** does it feel like music is playing just out of frame?

## wedding
*For: invitations and save-the-dates, RSVP sites, anniversary pages, proposals, registries, vow renewals.*
- **Scene:** the ceremony. Names that matter, a date that matters, everything pressed and chosen.
- **Color:** ivory `#FAF6EE` base with blush `#E8C4BC` and champagne gold `#C9A96A` as graces, deep foliage green `#3A5743` for weight; gold appears ONLY as hairlines and small ornaments, never as fills. (Rich variant for eastern ceremonies: deep maroon `#6B1F2A` + gold on cream - same rules, swapped anchors.)
- **Type:** the names are the design - a high-contrast elegant serif (**Cormorant Garamond**, **Instrument Serif**) with true italics for names at huge sizes; letterspaced small caps for dates and places. No script fonts - that rule inherits from `design-luxe`.
- **Shape:** the arch - image frames and section windows with fully rounded tops (`border-radius: 999px 999px 0 0`); 1px hairline frames with corner ornaments at most.
- **Space/pace:** ceremonial symmetry - centered composition is correct here (it's an aisle); 120–160px of breath; one moment per screen: the names, the date, the place.
- **Motion:** processional - slow fade-ups (600–800ms) with 100ms staggers, a hairline that draws across like a ribbon; nothing pops.
- **Texture:** cotton-paper grain at 3%, the faintest letterpress inset on display text, one or two pressed-flower illustration accents.
- **Copy:** formal warmth, names in full, dates spelled out. "Asha & Rohan - the fourth of June, two thousand twenty-seven." "We would be honored."
- **Kin:** `luxe` × `dreamy` with a thread of `nostalgic`.
- **Never:** script/calligraphy fonts, gold gradients, countdown timers (no urgency in ceremony), stock photos of rings and hands, emoji.
- **Litmus:** would it look right printed on thick cotton paper and held in two hands?

## interview
*For: portfolios, CV/resume sites, about-me pages, agency pitches, application microsites, speaker pages.*
- **Scene:** the first ten seconds across the table. Pressed shirt, firm handshake, one memorable true thing.
- **Color:** pressed neutrals - white or warm paper `#FAFAF7`, charcoal ink `#232629`; ONE deliberate accent worn like a good tie (oxblood `#6E2B2B`, racing green `#1F4D3A`, or cobalt `#2B4FD8`) on exactly three things: the name, the CTA, link hovers. Nothing else gets color.
- **Type:** impeccable hierarchy - the name at 64–96px (you are the headline); a disciplined grotesque (**General Sans**) or sharp serif (**Erode**); tabular figures for dates; a strict 1.25 scale with zero exceptions - inconsistency reads as sloppiness here more than anywhere.
- **Shape:** pressed and aligned - 0–4px radius, real 1px borders, a grid visibly obeyed. Alignment is the outfit.
- **Space/pace:** the ten-second scan above the fold - name → role → three proof points → contact; depth below for those who stay; sections in the order of a well-structured answer.
- **Motion:** stillness as confidence - 150ms hover states, perhaps one underline draw on the name, nothing else. Decorative movement reads as fidgeting.
- **Texture:** none. Pressed flat.
- **Copy:** first person, verifiable, zero filler - every claim carries a number, a name, or a link. "Shipped the checkout rewrite - conversion +18%." Never "passionate," "ninja," or "results-driven."
- **Kin:** `trustworthy` × `design-minimal`, warmed by exactly one personal note.
- **Never:** skill-percentage bars (90% of what?), star ratings on yourself, buzzword clouds, stock handshake photos, autoplay, logo walls without context.
- **Litmus:** after ten seconds, would they remember one specific true thing about you?

## thesis
*For: research write-ups, whitepapers, data journalism, case studies, methodology pages, published RFCs.*
- **Scene:** the defense. Every claim about to be cross-examined; the evidence within reach.
- **Color:** examination-room sober - paper `#FBFAF7`, ink `#1F2328`, ONE evidence accent (deep blue `#1F4B8E`) used exclusively on data, links, and citations, so that *colored = verifiable*; figures get a consistent 2–3 color categorical palette and nothing else does.
- **Type:** a working serif (**Source Serif 4**, **STIX Two Text**) with true small caps for section labels; mono for data and units; tabular figures wherever numbers appear; everything numbered - sections (3.2), figures (Fig. 4), tables.
- **Shape:** ruled - horizontal hairlines structure the page like a ledger of claims; boxes only around figures; 0–2px radius.
- **Space/pace:** abstract first (the whole argument in 150 words at the top), then a repeating claim → evidence → citation rhythm; the margin reserved for notes; density medium - rigor isn't cramped.
- **Motion:** none decorative - only functional (a 200ms chart transition when toggling datasets). Print-ready is the bar.
- **Texture:** none. Paper is texture enough.
- **Copy:** hedged exactly as much as the data demands and no more - "increases (p < 0.01)," not "skyrockets"; a limitations section is required; every figure caption states its takeaway.
- **Kin:** `design-scholarly`'s structure-minded sibling - scholarly sets the typographic register, thesis sets the argumentative one; use both for full academic work.
- **Never:** marketing superlatives near data, unlabeled axes, truncated y-axes, decorative chartjunk, claims without citations, "studies show" without naming the study.
- **Litmus:** could a hostile examiner reach the evidence behind any claim in two clicks?
